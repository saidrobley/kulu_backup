Farming Project

Kadugli County has granted Kulu 10,000 acres of farmland for farming practice. The goal of our project is to provide meals for school children because most of them often leave homes early in the morning without eating breakfast and walk far distances to attend classes. The schools don’t have meal programs either. This feeding program will make the learning environment more favorable for kids. We also want to provide rations to teachers and school employees. The county is committed to granting us more land as long as the program continues. In the slideshow images, you can see our farm-workers are sowing sorghum (grain) in July 2018.

Education Project

The conflict has caused an immense disruption and a tremendous destruction of educational infrastructures and facilities in the Nuba Mountains. Some students have crossed the border to South Sudan to attend schools in refugee camps, but the majority of them got cut off in the caves with their families. Most teachers have followed their students to refugee camps where they can teach while receiving rations corresponding to the salaries they could have earned. This situation is contributing to the growing illiteracy, which is disheartening. In the slideshow is our 8th grade students in Kadugli County. We’re planning to open a high school for them in 2021.

Health Project

Healthcare has always been insignificant program in the government services particularly in the marginalized states. Nuba Mountains; for example, has been denied the immunization campaign since 2011. The former government of Al Bashir bombed and destroyed most hospitals and clinics in areas under the administration of the SPLM-N; consequently, thousands people have died. We are building a new facility in Sangali Hospital that consists of three rooms: an operation, a labor, and a waiting room. Hopefully this project will be done by March 31, 2020. See the slideshow images.

Water Project

Clean drinking water has been a critical issue for the marginalized inhabitants since the establishment of the state of Sudan. People walk far distances to fetch stagnant dirty water from lakes, polluted reservoirs or streams for drinking. This is one of injustices that people of the Nuba Mountains have been enduring for a very long time. Many people die each day from diseases associated with lack of clean drinking water. In the slideshow images, there is a boy drinking from the stagnant water and two girls fetching dirty water from the stream for drinking. Our long-term goal is to initiate the water supply network by drilling wells and installing hand or engine water pumps so that the residents are able to access to clean drinking water.
